Common questions

Buyer Questions Answered

Questions buyers frequently ask. Answered by AISHA, our AI property advisor.

Why is Al Marjan Island the highest yielding area in UAE?

The Wynn Al Marjan Island integrated resort (UAE's first casino resort, opening 2027) is creating an unprecedented tourism demand spike for the area. Short-term rental rates are projected to reach AED 800–2,500/night once the resort opens — driving 8–10% yields even at current prices, with further upside anticipated.

Is Al Marjan Island freehold for foreign buyers?

Yes. Al Marjan Island is a designated freehold zone in Ras Al Khaimah. All nationalities can purchase with full title deed registration. RAK Land Department (RAKLD) manages all registrations.

Does Al Marjan Island property qualify for the UAE Golden Visa?

Yes. Properties at AED 2M+ qualify for the UAE 10-year Golden Visa through the national Golden Visa programme — it is not limited to Dubai. Many 2-bedroom and above units on Al Marjan Island qualify.

Is Al Marjan Island better than Dubai Marina for investment?

For pure yield, Al Marjan significantly outperforms: 8–10% vs Marina's 6.8–7.4%. For capital appreciation, Al Marjan is also ahead at +28.5% vs +14.2%. The trade-off is liquidity — Dubai Marina has more active resale market. Al Marjan is a long-hold investment.

What are the best developers on Al Marjan Island?

Emaar, Aldar (Abu Dhabi's largest developer, now active in RAK), RAK Properties, and international developers Marriott-branded Autograph Collection are key players. The Wynn Hotels Collection is directly adjacent. AISHA can shortlist projects by budget and handover date.

When does Wynn Al Marjan Island open?

Wynn Al Marjan Island is projected to open in 2027. The resort will include the UAE's first casino, 1,500+ hotel rooms, world-class dining, and entertainment venues. Property prices on the island have already increased 28.5% year-on-year in anticipation.
